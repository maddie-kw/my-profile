A Defining Moment

Patient-Centered Care

During med school, I worked with a psychiatric patient from Afghanistan who had PTSD after witnessing the Taliban shoot pets. His trauma was deeply tied to animals, and he associated them with loss and fear. I connected him with animal shelter resources and volunteer opportunities, hoping it might help him start to rebuild a healthier relationship with animals.

Even though I didn't get to see how things turned out (it was inpatient, so our time was limited), that experience showed me how important it is in psychiatry to look beyond symptoms and get creative with care that addresses what really matters to each person.

What Matters to Me

Public health is a huge passion of mine. I had the incredible opportunity to pursue my Master of Public Health in Australia, which gave me a global perspective on health systems and the social determinants that shape mental wellbeing. That experience deepened my commitment to addressing mental health not just at the individual level, but at the community and systems level too.

When I'm not studying medicine, I love learning languages. There's something cool about connecting with people from different cultures. I've also been mentoring underserved students through the college application process since undergrad, which has been really meaningful to me. In my downtime, I'm really into cross stitching (it's weirdly meditative), baking things for friends and family, and hanging out with my fiancé.
