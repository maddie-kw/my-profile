A Defining Moment

▼

During med school, I worked with a psychiatric patient from Afghanistan who had PTSD after witnessing the Taliban shoot pets. His trauma was deeply tied to animals, and he associated them with loss and fear. I connected him with animal shelter resources and volunteer opportunities, hoping it might help him start to rebuild a healthier relationship with animals. Even though I didn't get to see how things turned out (it was inpatient, so our time was limited), that experience showed me how important it is in psychiatry to look beyond symptoms and get creative with care that addresses what really matters to each person.
